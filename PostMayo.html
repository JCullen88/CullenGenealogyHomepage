From: allethaire***worldnet.att.net
Hi. I am James Joseph Cullen, III, born in 1924 in Paterson, New Jersey. I hope my information might help anyone digging into the Cullen family.

I am a mechanical engineer with an MBA and retired from the civil service in 1986 after having served as a rocket scientist (no kidding!) for the US Navy. I married Phyllis Mae Johnson of Warwick, Rhode Island, in 1948. We had two children: a son, James Joseph Cullen IV, born in 1950, who was a chemical engineer/geologist/hydrologist; and a daughter, Holly Allethaire Cullen, born in 1955, who is a nurse practitioner and nursing instructor.

My father, James Joseph Cullen, Jr., was born in Suffern, New York, in 1888. He had many jobs during the Great Depression, including driving a milk wagon and working in a tool crib at Curtiss-Wright (not their competitor, Hamilton Standard, as I think has been reported elsewhere) propeller division. In 1921 my father married Mary Timchak of Johnstown, Pennsylvania. They had two children: my sister, Anita Cullen Barrett, and me. My dad died in New Jersey in 1968.

His father, James Joseph Cullen, Sr., was born in 1853 in Co. Mayo, Ireland. He emigrated to Suffern, New York where he was a telegraph operator for the Erie Railroad. He married Catherine Sweenan and they had 11 children, eight girls and three boys. I know the boys were Thomas, James (my dad), and Edward (a machine-gunner in World War I). Of the girls, I remember Tess (Theresa) and Alice and Katherine (or Catherine). James, Sr., died in 1920.


From: flyingcrown***earthlink.net
My name is James Joseph Cullen, IV. I'm a civil engineer and geologist from Las Vegas, NV. My father was James Joseph Cullen, III, born in 1924 in Patterson, NJ, married Phyllis Mae Johnson from Warwick, RI. He was a mechanical engineer -- civil service for the U.S. Navy. My paternal grandfather was James J. Cullen, II. I no nothing of his birthplace but believe it to be in New York or New Jersey. He held various jobs through the Great Depression, including driving a milk wagon and working in a tool crib at Hamilton-Standard. He married a Czechoslovakian woman named Maria Timchak. James II and Maria also had a daughter, my aunt, whose name escapes me. She had two children (my cousins) by a husband John Barrett: Patti and John Barrett. Of my Great Grandfather, James Joseph Cullen (the first?) I know little except that he emigrated from Co. Mayo, Ireland to the United States, came from a very large family, and was a telegraph operator for the New York Central Railroad.
