From: hoodgpjrag***netscapeonline.co.uk
I'm researching a shipwreck in 1792 where two of the survivors were Mrs. Isabella Cullen and her younger daughter Margaret Frederica. Isabella was the eldest daughter of Sir Archibald Hope of Craighall, and about 32 at the time of the wreck. In 1778 she married Lieut William Cullen (who probably came from the Peterculter area) who subsequently went onto half pay and then left the army, probably to go to India as an Indigo planter. Isabella lost her elder daughter, name unknown, in the wreck, but she and Margaret finally reached India in early 1794, and there she rejoined William and they had various children whose birth dates I found at the IOLR/British Library. Margaret married the son of an east indiaman captain, Locke, in 1809.

William Cullen rejoined the army out in India, became a Colonel in the Scots Brigade in 1801. Isabella had returned to England in 1799, and outlived her husband by many years, dying in Edinburgh in 1842. If anyone can help get a bit of flesh on the bones of who these Cullens were, I would be very grateful and would willingly share what I know.

From: Jim Roache: jfroache***sympatico.ca
My maternal grandfather was Dan (Donnie) McCauley from Arranmore Island off County Donegal. His first wife, who died in childbirth in Broxburn, Scotland was a Coll whose family was also from the Island. But she had been born in Broxburn where there were shale mines and works to produce oil....manned mostly by Irish immigrants who lived in company housing until they went on strike and were thrown out on the streets.

After she died, Dan re-married, in a civil ceremony, a Polish lady he had met. They subsequently divorced, without offspring.

There is some suggestion that, in his final days, he resided with a Sarah (McCormack) Cullen at 166 Hospital Street, Glasgow. She was named correspondent in his divorce from his second wife, Magdalina, who nevertheless seems to have qualified for his widow's pension from the City of Glasgow where he was foreman, tramway overhead linesman, according to his death certificate (Feb 1944).

He died under mysterious circumstances. We would like to track anyone from that area and era. Perhaps Sarah Cullen had children who might still be alive and might be able to answer some questions including why she didn't get the pension.

Update: New info on Sarah McCormack Cullen -- she married John Duffy Cullen, Leith, 1917, is documented to have lived at the same addresses as Dan McCauley in Leith 1938-9, then Glasgow until 1944, alleged cohabitee. At least one child, Felix Michael Cullen, b 1923.
