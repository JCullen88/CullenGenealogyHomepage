Historical Notes on the
Pedigree of the Cullen Family
various sources as noted

David Cullen, of Lackagh Lodge, Co Leitrim, wrote the Cullen Family Pedigree in about 1860. An appended update has been written by a direct descendant, Robert A. Cullen, of South Africa. The Cullen Family Pedigree has been an invaluable source of information on the Cullens of Manorhamilton and others descended from Captain John Cullen. Captain John Cullen arrived in Ireland from Scotland about the year 1648. Since the Pedigree was first presented by Robert Cullen, I've spent some time following up on some clues within the text and trying to learn more about the background of the times and of the area where the family settled in Co Leitrim. These and other notes will be posted here and will hopefully better our understanding of this significant portion of the Cullen Family History.



The Wynnes of Sligo-Leitrim

One of the earliest families associated with the Cullens of Manorhamilton was the Wynne family, descendants of Owen Wynne, who died in 1670. This family is discussed in "The Wynnes of Sligo & Leitrim", a book by Winston Guthrie-Jones published in Ireland in 1994. The Wynnes are a Welsh Family and, according to the author, the family in Co Leitrim descends from Owen Wynne I, who arrived in Ireland from Wales and acquired lands around Lurganboy in Co Leitrim in about 1658. Owen became the founder of the family of Wynnes of Hazelwood in Co Sligo, the traditional seat of the Irish Wynnes. Owen's father was Lewis Gwynne of the family in Wales seated at Plas-yn-dre house, on the northern shore of Lake Bala, in Merioneth county (now part of Gwynedd county).

The Right Honorable Owen Wynne IV is the Owen Wynne mentioned in Robert Cullen's Pedigree. Patrick Cullen (abt1753 - 1775) was married to Judith Wynne, one of the daughters of Owen Wynne IV. You can find Patrick's place in the family tree on the Cullens of Manorhamilton page. According to the Cullen Family Pedigree, "Young Patt was married to Judith Anne Wynne daughter of the Right Honourable Owen Wynne of Haslewood in the County of Sligo, and Aunt to the present Right Honourable John Wynne - he was a very superior man in every way but died young leaving one daughter - since dead". According to the Guthrie-Jones publication, Owen had divided 10,000 pounds equally between his six sons and three daughters, except for Judith who "married without his privy, consent or approbation and to disoblige him." Evidently, Owen Wynne held some grudge against Patrick Cullen. This may have been due to religious differences or possibly due to an earlier loan that Patrick Cullen had made to the Wynnes. The repayments on this loan (7,300 pounds) have been described as "very severe".
