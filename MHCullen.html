The Cullens of Manorhamilton
Co. Leitrim, Ireland
David Evans, Robert Cullen, Jim & Pam Bruen, and many others
This is a special section of the Cullen Genealogy Homepage dealing with the descendants of the Cullens of Manorhamilton, a prominent family in Co Leitrim, Ireland from the mid-17'th to the mid-19'th centuries. The family was founded by one Captain John Cullen who came to Ireland with Cromwell in 1648. According to the family history, John Cullen was one of the family that had been evicted from Co Wexford and, for a time, lived in Scotland. This is likely where the connection to the Cullens of Nottinghamshire, England is to be found. Richard Cullen, who founded the "Rude Forefathers" line of Cullens and who died in Upton, Nottinghamshsire in 1579, had children with names that are all but exclusive to Scotland. The exact connection between the two brances of the Cullen family is the subject of ongoing research. One of the researchers, David Evans, a meteorologist for the Australian government and a descendant of Cullen relatives in Co Leitrim, has been tireless in his search for information on his ancestors in Ireland. He has gone so far as to personally visit the area and root out more information on his own and he has been rewarded with a few discoveries and much information concerning his ancestors. David was kind enough to make the information available to the rest of us in this area of the site.

Though David has done extensive research on the Cullen families in Co Leitrim, he has thus far been unable to connect his own ancestors into the known family tree. His specific branch is as follows:

Additional Pages of Cullen Data

  • There is a lost line of Cullens in Co Leitrim that is said to have originated in Co Wexford approx. mid-1700's. Very little progress has been made on this family. It is said that these Cullens are the ancestors of the "Charlie, Mickie, Charlie" Cullens. There are several researchers working on this project already!

  • Here is an excellent timeline concerning the names Soden and Cullen in Co's Sligo & Leitrim. The Soden family is related through the marriage of Rev Cairncross Cullen (~1754-1807) to Elizabeth Soden. These excellent record extracts have been thoughtfully put together and forwarded by a Soden descendant in Dublin who has done quite a bit of research into the Soden family. Note the dates and sources he has used in putting this page together - there are other sources of family information besides the BMD information and the Soden page is a good example of what can be possible.

  • Normally, I don't cross-link sections of my site but I've made an exception here. In the Related Irish Families section, there is a Pedigree of the Cullen Family written in about 1860 by David Cullen of Lackagh Lodge. The very early origins of the family are outlined and the later information was updated in 1990 by Robert Cullen of Johannesburg, South Africa. The above link will take you to that document. You may follow the links from there to get to the Related Irish Families page or you may use your back button to return here.

  • A page with information on the book The Sligo-Leitrim World of Kate Cullen 1832-1913 by Hilary Pyle. The book is an enlightening peek at 19'th century Protestant Ireland through the eyes of Kate Cullen, great-granddaughter of Patrick Cullen and Isabella Nesbitt of Skreeney, Manorhamilton. Just imagine how much more of a treasure this book would have been if Hilary had known back in 1997 about the Cullen Pedigree manuscript held by Bob Cullen of South Africa! Maybe a future project?... or maybe perhaps one of you out there reading this...

  • Dayan Goodsir Cullen has forwarded information on another branch of the Cullen family of Manorhamilton, Co Leitrim. These are the descendants of Valentine Blake Cullen (link has been temporarily disabled on request), mentioned in the 1860 Pedigree of the Cullen Family. Members of this family were active in the British Indian Army. The family has preserved letters and other documents outlining their genealogy, military history in the family, and some details concerning land ownership. Very interesting reading.

  • Jim Bruen and daughter Pam have prepared a page of birth record extracts for the surname Cullen in Manorhamilton. Years covered are from 1864 to 1909, with breaks in the years 1867 and (1892-1897). Source info for the LDS films are included. Parent's names are absent but will be updated over time.

  • There is also a page with additional information on the Cullen families of Co. Leitrim extracted from the Mormon IGI. The records include only those events not already covered in the family information sent by David Evans.

  • Here's another page of record transcriptions forwarded by David Evans. Some Cullen data he collected while in Ireland (lucky dog) has been typed in and e-mailed for posting to his new data page. If this keeps up we're all going to start thinking Cullen is an Australian name! (and what's wrong with that?). Lately also, I've included other items found or sent in which relate in one way or another to the Cullens in Co Leitrim.

  • Finally, from the data in Griffith's Valuation, I've posted a page that shows the distribution of Cullen landowners in Co Leitrim. There is a map and (soon) tables of the individual entries from Griffith's with notes on identified individuals.
