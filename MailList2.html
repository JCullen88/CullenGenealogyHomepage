Cullen Genealogy - List of Researchers

This is brand new so there is no one yet on the list. If you are interested in being included as a researcher, just e-mail me and asked to be added to the list of Cullen Researchers. The only requirement is that you provide a working e-mail or snail mail address at which you can be contacted. You should also provide a good-sized paragraph outlining your location and time period of interest. Unlike a query or posting, your interest should not lie in one individual or family. As a researcher, you would have an interest in whole groups or populations of individuals with the Cullen surname in a given area and be willing to receive or provide information regarding them. An example would be my own work on the Cullen families of Nottinghamshire, England or of Co Leitrim, Ireland. If your work has progressed and you are willing to share, I would be happy to create a set of pages on my site for your work if you do not yet have your own working webpage.

The E-Mail List for CULLEN researchers world-wide (may not be currently active):

This is a low volume list, run off a personal mail client, do not expect much in the way of complex capability, or performance.


Because it is run from a dial-up connection:
  • Messages will be forwarded when I dial up to go my email, at least once per day (exceptions: 1-2 days / month no sign-on).
  • Keep the messages reasonable in length (at least until I regain 56k with V.90).

When you send an e-mail to net.genealogy@ibm.net , with the word "CULLEN:" (exact case, including the colon, excluding quotes) in the subject line, the message will be distributed to all members on the list.


The email addresses of the list members will not show on the distributed messages.


To subscribe to this list automatically (and receive this wonderful welcome message):
  • Send an email message to net.genealogy@ibm.net
  • Subject must contain - "CULLEN-request:" (without the quotes).
  • The body must contain the word "subscribe" - the rest of the message is ignored.

To un-subscribe to this list automatically (and receive a wonderful goodbye message):
  • Send an email message to net.genealogy@ibm.net
  • Subject must contain - "CULLEN-request:" (without the quotes).
  • The body must contain the word "unsubscribe" - the rest of the message is ignored.

If you want to send an email to a person (me), send to net.genealogy with a subject that does NOT include the colon ":" character. In case it matters to you...nothing sent to this id is private, I will store it on a computer, and it could all go poof at any second. I promise nothing, all risk is yours.
