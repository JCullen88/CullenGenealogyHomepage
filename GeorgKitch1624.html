Will of George Kitchen, Husbandman of Upton
Written Feb 12, 1624, Proved Mar 1, 1624
Jim Cullen

The Kitchen/Kitching family is one that has deep and long respected standing in Upton. George Kitchen was a peer of Thomas Cullen the Churchwarden. In fact, they were brothers-in-law. George Kitchen was the brother of Agnes Kitchen whom Thomas Cullen married in 1593. George still calls Thomas his brother though Thomas Cullen's marriage to Agnes Kitchen ended in 1611 when Agnes passed away. I've included the will of George Kitchen for this reason and for the revealing of several other family connections as well. We now understand that Agnes had a brother George Kitchen and also another brother William Kitchen of Rolleston. One of the witnesses to the will of George Kitchen is Thomas Cullen and we see that, once again, Thomas did not sign his signature but placed his mark (TC). We seee the same circle off friends, families, and neighbors that we see in nearly every document having to do with the Cullen family.

There is a word I have left in brackets, [neuie], which is a word describing a relationship. Loosely it means neice (or nephew) but it can also be a term applied to other relationships as well. These terms were a little loose as compared to our understandings today. Notice that George's inventory totals about £29 which is a reasonable amount for a Husbandman or Yeoman in that area at that time. Though Thomas Cullen and George Kitchen were peers, the relative wealth of the Cullen family is remarkable. Thomas Cullen's inventory totalled about £182 in 1628.


Will of George Kitching, Husbandman of Upton, Nottinghamshire
Written Feb 12, 1624/25 & Probated Mar 1, 1624/25


In the name of God Amen, upon the twelfth day of February in the year of our Lord God One thousand Six hundred twentie and foure, I George Kitching of Upton neare Southwell in the Countie of Nottingham yeoman, sicke in bodie but of a good and perfect remembrance god bee thanked for the same dothe ordain & make this my last will & testament in manner and forme following. First I bequeath my soule into the hands of almighty god my maker, & my Bodie to be buried in the church yard of Upton at the disposall of my executors, and as for my temporall goods I give and bequeath as followeth. First I bequeath to Alce Taylor my [neuie?] One seeled bed with the best paire of sheetes with a Coverlet & a Blanket, a seeled chiste with a table and the frame, and my cobberd, & my brass pott, and my bigest brass kettell, with all the pewter dishes candelstickes and saltes. Item I give and bequeath unto Thomas Kitching my [neuie] my redd doued cow, and foure strikes of Barley. Item I give & bequeath unto my Brother Thomas Cullen Children my two redd calves, and to himselfe, my [least] blacke bald hefer, and foure strikes of barley. Item I give unto Johne Chappill my [neuie] my yonge black cow. Item I give unto Humfray Hallam two swine pigges. Item I give unto George Pride of Upton two strikes of barley, and to his yongest sone two swine piges, and to his eldest sone One strike of barley, and one strike of pease, and to his daughter my bigest brass kettell save one, and foure shillinges of money. Item I give and bequeath unto Richard Taylor of Sutton uppon trent, foure strikes of barley, & two strikes of pease. Item I give unto my Coson William Taylor my two baron stirkes and my best sow. Item I give unto Emat Keppitt the least pigg of the five. Item I give and bequeath unto my brother William Kitching of Rowlston deceased unto six of his eldest Children the some of six poundes to bee devided by equall portions, to be paid unto theme at the end, and terme of two yeares after my decease. All the rest of my goods unbequeathed I give & bequeath unto my Coson Thomas Taylor whome I make & ordaine full executor of this my last will, and Testament, my debts beeing paid and my funerall expences being discharged.

In witnes wherof I have hereunto sett my hande the day and yeare within written

George (his mark) Kitching

Witnesseth: George Bilbie, Thomas (TC his mark) Cullen,
George (GK his mark) Bykerk, Humfray (I his mark) Hallam

$1.$ Martij 1664. Coram M[agist]ro ffelder Com[missar]io etc.Probat[um] per Geo: Bilby et Thomam Cullen testes etc.onus ex[ecutionis] vi perso[naliter] jurat[is] salvo etc. ext[rahendo] inve[ntariu]m pro plenoetc. prestitaq[ue] cautione ffidejussoria etc.

(Note on the date: The scribe writes "1664" using the old style legal calendar where the new year began on March 25th. In our modern historical calendar, this date is March 1, 1665).

Modern English Translation:
1 March 1664 [/65]. Before Master ffelder, Commissioner, etc.Proved by George Bilby and Thomas Cullen, witnesses, etc. The burden of execution [of the will] was sworn by them personally, saving [the right of others], an inventory to be extracted for the full amount, etc., and a security bond having been provided, etc.

Will Inventory of George Kitching, Husbandman of Upton, Nottinghamshire


A Tru Inventorie of all the good and chattelles of George Kitching of Upton near Southwell, in the countie of Nottingham husbandman deceased Vewed & praised the last day of ffebruarie in the yeare of our lord god 1624 by Thomas Cullen George Bilbie George Bykerk & Robert Gibson

Item: His purse, his girdell, and apparell — 13s 4d
Item: In the Hall, one table standing of a frame, with a forme and binche — 8s
Item: One cubberd, a chaire, and stools — 13s
Item: Three kittes bowles, dishes, and other woodden ware — 3s 4d
Item: The fire ern recones tonges skummer brandrith pothookes, and spitt — 2s
Item: All the pewter dishes, one candelstick, and a salt — 6s 4d
Item: Two brass pannes, a pott, and a little hingin pann — 15s

Item: One seeled chist — 5s
Item: Two pairs of other sheetes with other aparie ware — 5s
Item: Two looms, one barrill, two spinning wheeles, and some other implements — 6s 8d
Item: All the painted clothes — 12d
Item: In the Chamber, the hemp yarn and woole — 12s
Item: The winnowed barley and pease — 32s
Item: One half flick of bacon — 2s
Item: All the unthrashen corne — 40s
Item: All the hay — 20s
Item: The firewood and compose or menure in the yarde — 5s
Item: One litle hange house and swine coate — 6s 8d
Item: Six kyne (cows) — 12L
Item: Three lesser heifers — 3L
Item: Two sowes and five pigges — 20s
Item: One cocke and the hennes — 2s
Item: Five roodes of barley earthe — 26s 8d
