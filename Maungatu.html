The Cullen Family of
Maungaturoto, New Zealand
From the family history collection of Cliff Cullen (Aukland, NZ)
If you are introduced as a Cullen in New Zealand, it is inevitable that you will be asked if you are of Maungaturoto. This branch of the Cullen family has grown considerably since the founding father, John Ash Cullen, arrived there back in 1863. The extended family now includes over 2000 individuals. Cliff Cullen has documented this family in book form, complete with photographs and document reproductions, from Richard Cullen the first of the Rude Forefathers abt1560, all the way down to the present day.

It would be impossible to reproduce Cliff's monumental work here in its entirety (over 400 pages) so I have collected the known male lines of this branch starting with the emigrant ancestor, John Ash Cullen. If you are interested in the ancestry of John Ash Cullen, read the article on the Cullens of Eastwood, Nottinghamshire on the English relatives page; that history ties John's line into that of the Rude Forefathers.

If you would like to order the complete Cullen Family History in book form, it is available from Evagean Publishing, 266 Shaw Road, Titirangi, Aukland, New Zealand. Prices are (and this may not be current): $190 Super De-luxe leather bound, $125 Deluxe Edition hardcover, $88 Standard Edition soft cover. Shipping charges apply. The prices may seem steep, but remember that the book runs over 400 pages, including photographs and reproductions of the wills of the early Cullens (1575-1805) in Nottinghamshire.

There are photos of some of the members of the New Zealand family of Cullens on a website put up by Ralph Cullen, son of Malcolm Cullen. (See Malcolm's place in the family tree below). Ralph has solid roots in New Zealand and, as you'll see, has carried on the traditional family values. Please take the time to visit Ralph Cullen's Web Site.

Brief Biography of John Ash Cullen

John Ash Cullen was born at Fiskerton, Nottinghamshire, on January 9,1813, to parents Benjamin and Esther (Ash) Cullen. John's christening was at St. Mary's, Upton, on March 15, 1813. He likely worked in the lace-making business run by his father because, at the age of 18, John designed machinery for making lace and had intended to patent the design. Another inventor beat him to the patent and so John deserted this occupation for another. He may have worked on his Uncle Gervase's farm in Lincolnshire since his future wife's family lived in the area. John and Sabina (Bee) Baldock were the parents of Susannah Baldock (John's future wife); the entire Baldock family emigrated from Lincolnshire to Australia in 1840 aboard the Fairfield. Some time later, John Ash also emigrated to Australia as he was married to Susannah Baldock at the Adelaide Congregational Church in Australia on November 5,1844. At the time of his marriage, John had been in Australia at least three years as a farmer at Morpeth Vale.

John and Susannah continued life at Morpeth Vale and had three children there; Henry (1845), Esther (1847), and Sabina (1849). In 1851 gold was discovered at Forest Creek and the Cullen family followed this pursuit. Their next two children were; Benjamin born 1851 in Gawler Town, and Mary Anne born 1853 in Forest Creek. Once again John had his hard work taken from him; their prospecting partner stole away in the middle of the night with their share of the gold. Dismayed, the family returned to England in 1853.

Soon after this, John and his growing family headed overseas again and settled as farmers at Guelph, Ontario, Canada. The piece of land they farmed was long known as Cullen's Farm. They remained there nearly ten years and had four more children; John born 1856 at Blythe Hulleth, William (1859), Sarah (1860), and Thomas (1862). These last three children were all born at Morris. Ill health of one of the children saw them return once more to England.

Very soon afterwards, the family once again ventured overseas, this time aboard the Tyburnia, bound for Aukland, New Zealand. They departed on a three month voyage on June 2,1863. There were many difficulties after their arrival on September 4,1863. Young Thomas, who had been so ill, died December 11'th at just over a year and a half old. The family obtained lands at Kaiwaka, 70mi north of Aukland. This was the family home for a year and a half until the homestead at Maungaturoto was ready for moving in. In 1865, after John and his sons had cleared the land and built a suitable home, the family moved in for permanent residence at Maungaturoto. By 1869, the family had increased in size; Elizabeth born 1864 at Mangawhai, Thomas (1866) and James Edward (1869) born at Maungaturoto.

At long last, John Ash was enjoying the fruits of his labor. The farming venture at Maungaturoto proved to be entirely successful and the family busied themselves with the farming effort and community works. Unfortunately, John's share in the happiness was cut short. John Ash Cullen died of cancer on June 6,1870 at the age of 57. Though he suffered much to provide a good life for his family and had fate slighting him at nearly every turn, the legacy of this amazing man has survived undiminished to this day. John Ash Cullen took the brunt of misfortune yet maintained determination and good faith in order to overcome. Due to his efforts and example, his descendants have enjoyed a prosperous life in New Zealand and no doubt look forward to an even brighter future.
