From: jcullen936***conknet.com
I'm looking for the ancestors of my grandfather, James C. Cullen, of West Stockbridge, Massachusetts. James C. and Honora Cullen are my immigrant ancestors. Note that Stockbridge and West Stockbridge are two distinct and separate Towns. In the early 1800's, West Stockbridge appears to have been a miners settlement. My grandparents are buried in St. Patrick's Cemetery, West Stockbridge:

  • James C. Cullen (grandfather): b.1845 or 1848 d.1920 m. 18Jun1868. A Blacksmith in the late 1800's in West Stockbridge. He or his wife's family (Crowe) were said to have owned a farm, in the late 1800's, in New Lebanon, NY, east of Albany, on the border with Massachusetts. Cullen relatives owned the farm until at least 1936. Family given names are John, James, Daniel, Thomas, Patrick. This side of the Cullen family had some members who spent time in New Lebanon, New York. An obiturary notice states that James C. Cullen was born in Co Limerick and family tradition states that he may have been from Co Cork. It is known that James' father's name was also James. Other children were (James C. was the first born): Patrick, Thomas, Michael, John , Mary and Margaret.

  • Honora Crowe (grandmother): b.1845 d. 1936. The obituary of Honora Crowe states that the pallbearers were six "Cullen" nephews, named Jean, John, Donald, James, Harold and William. There may also have been another nephew Edward. The obituary also states that Honora was born in Co Tipperary and, according to the present family, she may have lived in Co Galway at one point. Honora had brothers and sisters (Honora was the second born): Mary, Julie, Margaret, Alice, Walter, Michael, John, and Daniel.

Others Cullens buried in West Stockbridge, Massachusetts:

  • John C. Cullen "son of James Cullen": b. 30Jan1863 d. 16Sep1891 , "28 years, 9 months, 16 days". John is possibly a younger brother of the above James C. Cullen.
  • Patrick Sarsfield Cullen: b.1835 d. 1911. Research indicates that Patrick is the older brother of James C. Cullen above.
  • Patrick's wife, Ellen: b.1844 d. 1910.

Another group of Cullens, assumed to be related to the above , are buried at St. Joseph's Cemetery, Pittsfield, Massachusetts:

  • Patrick Cullen: d. 22Nov1867 at "40 years old" (born then abt. 1827).
  • Mary Ann Cougan: (assumed to be Patrick's wife) d. 30Apr1863, "31 years old"
  • Margaret Laine Claffey: b. 2Jan1840 d. 27Jun1912 (buried with Patrick, too young to be his daughter. ?????).
  • Catherine Cullen: "wife of Michael Cullen" d. 6Jan1880, "65 years".
  • Sarah C. Cullen: d. 10Apr1858, "1 year".
  • Edward P. Cullen: d. 20Jan1861, "1 year".
  • Margaret T, Cullen: d. 30Apr1863, "6 months".
  • William J. Cullen: d. 7Jan1852, "6 months".
  • Mary E. Cullen: d. 7Jun1858, "2 years".
  • Eddy Cullen: d. 12Mar1873, "12 years".
  • John C. Cullen: b.16Jan1853 d. 29Apr1920. Possibly a brother to James C. Cullen who is buried in Stockbridge, Mass.

I would very much like to find out the ancestry of James C. Cullen (1848-1920), Blacksmith from West Stockbridge, Massachusetts. Any hints or information would be very much appreciated!
