Early Cullens of Upton Nottinghamshire
Miscellaneous Records Prior to 1600
Jim Cullen

This page will hold the miscellaneous records I've come across that demonstrate that there were Cullen families living in central Nottinghamshire prior to about 1600. These references will be little more than scraps with minimal information given to go along with them. These families, unknown until now, were contemporaries of Richard Cullen who died in Upton in 1580. While connections to the Cullens of Upton and surrounding areas are not known, it can be assumed there is some connection in some cases. On the other hand, there are known records of very early Cullens in neighboring Lincolnshire that I don't mention much on this site since there is just no evidence yet for their connection to the known Cullen families rooted in the Village of Upton.


Noncuperative Will of Katherine Robinson Feb 8, 1591


​This is a nuncupative will of Margaret Robinson in the probate court of Southwell written Feb 8, 1591 and probated Mar 18, 1591. It seems that Margaret may have suffered a sudden illness at a relatively young age based on the type of will and the state of the family. Margaret was a "spinster" and "late of Upton, Southwell". The appointed Executrix was her mother, Katherine Robinson. Now Robinson was a well known and prominent name is Upton in those days. The list of witnesses were: Robert Gibson, Thomas Cullen, Katherine Robinson, and Henrie Robinson. Now, it's not known if this Thomas Cullen is the son of Richard Cullen who died in 1580 in Upton but, if it was, he was about 25 years of age at the time. Now we come to the mentioned family members which is the really interesting part. Henrie Robinson is Margaret's brother. Marie Gibson is Margaret's sister. Marie "has family" which I take to mean is married to a Gibson, probably Robert Gibson in the list of witnesses. Five Gibson children are mentioned for this marriage. Margaret also had a brother Robert Cullen who was old enough at the time to have a son, John Cullen, who was also mentioned in the will. This puts Robert at the very least 21 years of age and so born prior to about 1570, making Robert a contemporary of Richard Cullen. There is no mention of a Robert Cullen in Richard's family so he must have been a nephew or possibly a cousin, if he was indeed related as we suspect. Even though Margaret's mother Katherrine was named as Executrix, after Margaret's death, her brother Henry Robinson was appointed to handle Margaret's estate and affairs. The reason for this could be that Margaret's mother Katherine was also ill, possibly from some disease that had struck the family. We see that immediately after the probate of Margaret Robinson, her mother Katherine died intestate and her probate appears on Mar 24, 1591. She is recorded as a widow late of Oxton, Nottinghamshire. Her son Henry Robinson was appointed to handle her estate and affairs.


Source: DGS#: 008073104, Image#: 266/277

Thomas Cullen, Trustee of Collie's Charity of Upton, 1580


I've simply copied this section from the Collie's Charity Page. Thomas Cullen, who is listed as a witness in 1580/81, appears to have been a contemporary of Richard Cullen. Richard died this same year and is buried at St Peter & St Paul's in the churchyard. Thomas Cullen, the son of Richard, was about fifteen at the time. Even with the uncertainty about his year of birth, Thomas was nowhere near the age where he could have been entrusted with the affairs of the village, especially with the legal and monetary issues the trustees must have handled at the time. Thomas Cullen the trustee could be a brother, cousin, or possibly an uncle of Richard. Richard's father, by 1580, was already passed on and was also buried in the Upton churchyard. It is curious that we find nearby in Rolleston, in the parish registers for Rolleston Holy Trinity, the marriage of Thomas Cullen and Alice Robinson on Sep 12, 1567. The time and place is uncanny and the name Robinson is very well known in and around Upton. To further the mystery, we also find another Alice Cullen who was married to George Kytchen at Upton St Peter & St Paul's on Jun 15, 1602. Alice is a name not seen that often so suspicion of a connection could be warranted. Her husband's surname, Kitchen, is certainly no stranger to Upton and the surrounding areas.
